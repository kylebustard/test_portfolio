About me.

I am a full-stack developer. I utilize test-driven development to build dynamic web applications using Ruby on Rails, React JavaScript, HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap. My preferred operating system is Mac OS X, but I also use Ubuntu/Linux on a virtual machine.


I have a passion for programming and learning new technologies. I have always been a detail-oriented individual with an aptitude for learning—I am a fast learner and a hacker.

As a child, my favorite school subject was history. In university I was a double major student of philosophy and sociology. I am fascinated by how small ideas can evolve into significant phenomenons and even institutions. I love sharing ideas, and learning from others.

I completed university courses that were popular with the computer science students (calculus, advanced logic, and the philosophy of language), but I didn't particularly have any affinity for technology—in fact, I didn't know how to type! At the urging of a friend, I gave programming a chance and fell in love with it. I soon enrolled in a full-time code immersion course and devoured all learning opportunities I could find.

BONUS: Here's a blog post I wrote as a code bootcamp student.

My methodology was both practical and immersive: I was teaching myself how to type while also learning to code. I began networking by going to developer meetups and touring local tech companies weekly. I listen to tech podcasts every time I drive my car. I have a neat bookmark folder system in my browser—but I still manage to keep nearly twenty tabs open to documentation and code-related blogs! I've instructed 3 different kids coding classes, and I've been a gateway/mentor for a couple of friends who've started coding. I've tutored code bootcamp students remotely via Google Hangout screensharing. Currently I'm taking a night course called "Startup Primer" where I meet twice weekly with other entrepreneurs and developers, and we practice our elevator pitches and hone business strategy.

I've thrived in the high-paced learning environment of a code bootcamp. In just a matter of weeks I learned how to program, learned cool technologies, built blogs, e-commerce, and social media apps. I like the fast-pace, and I've continued that working in a startup as well as helping my wife launch her fine art business. I will always be learning, and making my own contributions to the open-source community and the world!

I really don't like to talk about myself. I like writing on other subjects. Occasionally I blog at Medium. Checkout my writing on programming, hackathons, jazz, and humor.
